# Tawala Product System Documentation

This file defines the core alignment principles for building, designing, and positioning **Tawala** — a business management platform for Kenyan SMEs.

It ensures consistency across product, engineering, marketing, and UX decisions.

---

# 1. TAWALA-BRAND.md — How We Speak

## Core Identity

Tawala is a calm, confident, and empowering business partner.

We do not sound like software.  
We sound like control, clarity, and progress.

---

## Tone of Voice

### We are:

- Simple
- Direct
- Empowering
- Human
- Grounded in real biashara struggles

### We are NOT:

- Corporate
- Over-technical
- Flashy or hype-driven
- Overly complex
- “AI buzzword” heavy

---

## Language Style

### Preferred:

- “Tawala biashara yako”
- “Manage your business with ease”
- “From hustle to structure”
- “Stay in control of your biashara”

### Avoid:

- “Next-gen AI-powered POS engine”
- “Enterprise-grade distributed commerce infrastructure”
- Overuse of technical jargon

---

## Personality

Tawala feels like:

- a trusted assistant
- a disciplined business partner
- a calm operator in chaos

# 3. PRODUCT-PRINCIPLES.md — How We Build

## Core Philosophy

Tawala is built for real-world biashara environments in Kenya:

- low technical adoption
- high operational pressure
- fast-moving sales environments

---

## Product Principles

### 1. Simplicity over Features

If a feature adds complexity without value → remove it.

---

### 2. Speed is Trust

Every interaction must feel instant and reliable.

---

### 3. Offline-First Thinking (where possible)

Assume unstable networks in real deployments.

---

### 4. Real Business First

Every feature must map to:

- sales
- stock
- staff
- profit
- operations

If it doesn’t → it is not core.

---

### 5. Progressive Complexity

- Simple for beginners
- Powerful for advanced users

---

### 6. Data is the Product

Every action should improve business visibility.

---

## System Design Direction

- Modular architecture
- API-first backend
- Scalable multi-tenant design
- Auditability for transactions

---

# 4. UX-PRINCIPLES.md — How UI Should Feel

## Core UX Goal

Tawala UI should feel:

> calm, clear, and in control

The user should never feel overwhelmed.

---

## UX Principles

### 1. Clarity over Density

Avoid cluttered dashboards.

---

### 2. One Action Per Screen Focus

Each view should guide a primary action.

---

### 3. Visual Calmness

- soft colors
- consistent spacing
- minimal cognitive load

---

### 4. Familiar Patterns

Use interfaces that match:

- receipts
- ledgers
- simple dashboards
- familiar biashara workflows

---

### 5. Instant Feedback

Every action must confirm:

- success
- failure
- loading state

---

### 6. Trust Through Design

UI must feel:

- stable
- predictable
- reliable

---

## UI Language

Preferred:

- “Sales”
- “Stock”
- “Reports”
- “Transactions”

Avoid:

- abstract technical labels
- confusing enterprise jargon

---

## Interaction Philosophy

- Reduce clicks
- Reduce thinking time
- Reduce errors
- Increase confidence
